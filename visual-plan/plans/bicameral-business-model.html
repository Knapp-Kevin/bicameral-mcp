01 — Why This Works

The contradiction AI created

"The number of decisions required to build quality software does not decrease with AI. In many cases it increases, because velocity is higher and you're making more decisions in quick succession." — Jin Kuan, Bicameral positioning doc
"AI is essentially cannibalizing context discovery, which it so badly needs in order to function well. That is the contradiction we're dealing with." — Bicameral core thesis
"Load balancer to help with feature prioritization." — PM interview signal (Mar 17, 2026)
The AI paradox: Coding agents ship faster, so teams make more decisions per week. But more decisions = more context that needs to be captured, traced, and validated. Generic AI tools (Claude + Notion) handle authoring — they go deaf when code hits production.
The wedge: Communication is the new bottleneck (site headline). Bicameral gets in the door free, building the org's decision history. Once 3–6 months of decisions are tracked, the ledger becomes a proprietary data asset no competitor can replicate.
The moat: "Low hallucination and high recall — that's a really tough balance. That is where our moat is going to be." The ledger trains itself on the org's own vocabulary, constraints, and codebase patterns. It compounds with every meeting.

04 — Product Details

The two paid products

Paid — PM Tier
Feature Load Balancer
Transforms the decision ledger into a living feature priority signal. PMs stop guessing which features are safe to ship — they see exactly which are blocked by unresolved engineering decisions.
👩‍💼 Buyer: PM / Head of Product
  • Ranks backlog items by pending/drifted decision count — features with unresolved decisions get flagged before sprint commit
  • Blast radius overlay: "shipping checkout retry touches PaymentProcessor (4 decisions, 11 dependents)" → sprint risk score
  • Tech debt → feature velocity map: surfaces which areas of the codebase slow down delivery the most, backed by real commit evidence
  • Firefighting predictor: tracks which past "drifted" decisions became production incidents
  • Integrates into Linear/Jira: auto-comments on tickets with pending decisions that affect scope
Requires: Active decision ledger with 3+ months of ingested meetings. The signal quality scales directly with ledger depth — a 2-week-old ledger gives basic prioritization; a 6-month ledger gives near-perfect sprint risk prediction.
Paid — Dev Tier
The Translator
Sits in on live meetings. When engineers debate technical options, it real-time surfaces the business implications — drawn from the org's own decision history — so tradeoffs are grounded in evidence, not intuition.
👨‍💻 Buyer: Eng Manager / CTO
  • Real-time autocomplete: "switching to a queue here" → Translator surfaces 2 prior queue decisions, known reliability constraint, +1.5wk timeline impact from ledger history
  • Business ↔ technical translation: maps engineer-speak to PM-speak instantly ("horizontal scaling" → "handles 10x Black Friday traffic without service disruption")
  • Decision ante-mortem: before a decision is made, surfaces all historical decisions in the same code area and their outcomes (reflected / drifted / caused incident)
  • SDLC constitution: learns org-specific patterns ("payments always implies webhooks + retry + notification — here's what past devs missed")
  • Post-meeting decision capture: auto-proposes /ingest targets at meeting end, reduces friction of building the ledger
Requires: Active decision ledger + meeting audio access (Granola, Zoom, or similar). The org-specific SDLC constitution only emerges after sufficient ledger depth — the Translator gets meaningfully smarter at months 2, 4, and 8 of usage.
